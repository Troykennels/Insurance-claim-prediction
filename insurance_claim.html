sns.countplot(x='Claim', data=insurance_data)
plt.title("Claim Distribution")
plt.show()

Claim - Insight

Insight: The distribution of the target variable shows a clear class imbalance, with a substantially higher number of buildings without claims compared to those with at least one claim. This indicates that insurance claims are relatively rare events in the dataset, which is consistent with real-world insurance scenarios. As a result, accuracy alone would be a misleading evaluation metric, and metrics such as recall, precision, and ROC-AUC are more appropriate for assessing model performance.

Building Type vs Claim Insight

Certain building types exhibit a higher proportion of claims relative to others, suggesting that structural characteristics and usage patterns associated with building type influence insurance risk. This indicates that Building_Type is an important categorical feature and should be retained in the model to capture variations in claim likelihood across different building categories.

Building Dimension Distribution Insight

Insight: The distribution of building dimensions is right-skewed, with most buildings having relatively small dimensions and a smaller number of very large buildings forming a long tail. This suggests heterogeneity in building sizes within the dataset. Larger buildings may be associated with higher exposure and potential claim severity, making building dimension a relevant numerical predictor. Feature scaling is therefore necessary to ensure this variable contributes appropriately during model training.

In[]:
# this is to create preprocessing pipelines for both numerical and categorical data
numeric_transformer = Pipeline(steps=[
    ("scaler", StandardScaler())
])

categorical_transformer = Pipeline(steps=[
    ("onehot", OneHotEncoder(handle_unknown="ignore"))
])

preprocessor = ColumnTransformer(
    transformers=[
        ("num", numeric_transformer, numerical_features),
        ("cat", categorical_transformer, categorical_features)
    ]
)
